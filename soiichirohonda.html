Soichiro Honda is the founder of Honda. Born in Japan, he had a interest in engineering from a young age. In the 1920s, he worked as a mechanic and later created his own car repair shop. Honda's passion led him to develop a piston ring that greatly improved the efficiency of internal combustion engines. Then he established Tokai Seiki, a company focused on manufacturing these piston rings, which eventually became a successful company. In 1948, he make his dream alive by establishing Honda Motor Corporation with a focus on motorcycles. His first motorcycle is launched in 1958, which revolutionize the motorcycle industry with its affordability and reliability.

Honda then started to expand beyond motorcycles, Honda started to get into the automotive car sector. In 1963, the Honda S500 sports car was launched, which then Honda also created the Civic in the 1970s, which has a compact size, great fuel efficiency, and environmental consciousness. Honda's Passion for racing also led him to invent the VTEC variable valve timing.
