Kiichiro Toyoda is the founder of Toyota Motor Corporation. Born in Japan, his dad is Sakichi Toyoda. In the 1930s, Kiichiro Toyoda realizes the potential of the car industry and thought to change his family business. He traveled to the United States to study the car manufacturing process and understand the principles behind mass production. Inspired by what he learned, he returned to Japan with the vision of creating a Japanese car manufacture company.

In 1937, Toyoda founded Toyota Motor Corporation, and his first car is produced in 1936. The company's few first years were challenging because of World War 2, Toyoda's Brilliant but Effective Strategic decisions helped solve these difficulties. Kiichiro Toyoda's hardwork and many innovation led Toyota to become one of the world's largest car manufacturers. He served as the president of the company until 1950 and then remained on the board of directors until his death in 1952.
