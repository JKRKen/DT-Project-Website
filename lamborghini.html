Ferruccio Lamborghini was born on April 28, 1916, near Cento, Italy, which is the founder of Lamborghini. Before Ferrucio Lamborghini ventured into the world of automotive, Lamborghini built a successful tractor-manufacturing business on the post-World War II. His skills in mechanical engineering and innovation in tractor design made him wealthy and have a good reputation. In the early 1960s, Ferruccio Lamborghini is a sport car enthusiast, which decided to buy a Ferrari, but then he found out that the Ferrari he owned had brake issues after he bought it, which he eventually took into the Ferrari repair shop. Enzo Ferrari refused to fix his issues then insults him, which fed up Ferrucio Lamborghini. He then decided to built his own car, and founded Automobili Lamborghini in 1963, aiming to create superior sports cars that has power, luxury, and reliability combined, and with the goal to beat Ferrari.

Lamborghini's first production car was the 350 GT, which is his commitment to the innovation and quality. Then Lamborghini sets up to build the legendary Miura. Ferruccio was also known because of his attention to detail. In the 1970s, Lamborghini got into financial problem and sold most of his stocks in the company. However, Lamborghini continued to produce some of the world's most iconic sports cars, like the Countach, Diablo, and Aventador.
